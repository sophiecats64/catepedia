Cat Nutrition

like humans, cats need a well balanced diet, that is centered around their breed, age and overall fitness. unlike humans, your cat doesn't need leafy greens or fruit, instead they should be kept on a primarily meat diet.

Cats should also not be given treats often, more once a day for small treats. this ensures your cat is healthy, but also happy.

Wet/dry food

While both have their uses, it depends on how your cat and household, which one you should choose. or, even better, you could incorperate a half-in-half diet for your cat.

Wet Food

This food is high in water content, which helps cats get enough hydration, as most do not get enough via drinking.

Dry Food

This is easy to store, simple to serve. You can easily leave this food in the bowl and it won't spoil, allowing your cat to graze whenever they are hungry. When it is properly formulated, it can also help with your cats dental health

Cat Toys

When cats are bored, they tend to get stressed, which leads to behavior issues, to help with this, you need to ensure that they have plenty of enrichment and toys.

Toys tend to get boring for most cats, so ensure that they are regularly swapped around, to keep them exciting and new. You can also spend time bonding with your cats by playing with them.

Food Puzzles

Start easy, and with a high-value treat. When your kitten gets the hang of things, increase the difficulty level, and perhaps mix other food in with the treats. Many cats are even fed their meals using food puzzles and puzzle feeders!

Homemade Toys

most cat toys are expensive to buy, however, don't let that stop you! you can easily create cat toys out of cardboard, or other scrap items you have around the house. Use your creativity, or you can easily search up tutorials online.

Cat Grooming

Cats need to be groomed regularly, however, how often is dependant on how long the cats fur is, for example longhaired cats, need to be brushed twice a week, whereas short haired cats can get away with once a week, or every two weeks.

This can normally be done at home, using cat brushes and other items, which you can easily get at pet stores, however, sometimes they will be too matted or dirty to be done at home, this is when you can take them to a groomer, to get them done for you.
