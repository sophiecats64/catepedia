Quick Facts

  1. The International Cat Association currently recognizes 73 breeds of cats.
  2. There are over 600 million cats in the world today, including pet, stray, and feral cats.
  3. The average domestic indoor cat’s lifespan is 10–15 years.
  4. Heart disease is the leading cause of sudden death in cats.
  5. Cats spend up to 50% of their waking hours grooming themselves.

History of cats

The scientfic name for cats is Felis Catus, and is then subdivided into two families. Pantherinae, which raor (or better known as big cats) and felinae which purr (better known as small cats).

We can trace their ancestry to wildcats (Felis silvestris lybica) in the Near East around 9,000 years ago. it is likely that cats began living near humans to hunt rodents attracted to grain stores.

Cats were first domesticated in the Fertile Crescent. Their independent nature made them different from other domesticated animals, as they largely retained their wild instincts.

Cats were revered in ancient Egypt around 4,000 years ago. They were associated with the goddess Bastet, symbolizing protection, fertility, and home life. Killing a cat was considered a grave crime.

Cats spread to Europe, Asia, and Africa through trade and exploration. In ancient Rome, they were valued for pest control and companionship.

During the Middle Ages, cats were both revered and feared. They were sometimes associated with superstition and witchcraft, leading to persecution. (primarily of black cats) This decline in cat populations may have contributed to the spread of the Black Plague, as rodent populations increased.

In the 18th and 19th centuries, cats regained popularity as pets. Selective breeding began, leading to the development of distinct breeds. Today, cats are among the most popular pets worldwide.

Cats have held diverse symbolic meanings across cultures, from good luck in Japan (Maneki-neko) to mystical associations in folklore.

Irish Folklore about cats

  • A traditional Irish greeting on entering a house was; ‘God save all here, except the cat’, this was likely due to a cats association with the devil.
  • it was believed to be lucky for a stray cat to enter the house.
  • it was thought that to be looked at fixedly by a cat after it had washed its face was a sign of approaching marriage.
  • to be looked at fixedly by a cat, except after washing its face, was an omen of sickness or death.
  • To dream of a cat foretold an enemy; to dream of a dog a friend.
  • a purring cat or a cat with its back to the fire was a sign of rain.
  • it was an omen of bad luck if a cat caught a mouse and allowed it to escape for no reason.
  • it was thought that putting a cat under a pot brought bad weather and it was apparently a common practice among sailors’ wives in order to keep their men at home.
